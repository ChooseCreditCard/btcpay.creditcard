How BTC becomes a card transaction at the terminal.

Compare Bitcoin-Friendly Cards

What is a Bitcoin card?

A Bitcoin-linked card lets you pay anywhere Visa or Mastercard is accepted, while holding BTC as the underlying value. When you tap/swipe, the provider converts just enough BTC to cover the purchase.

  • • You spend like a normal card
  • • Merchant receives local currency
  • • Provider auto-sells BTC at transaction time
  • • Some cards offer BTC cashback

How BTC-to-card flow works

1. You tap the card

NFC or chip sends tokenized card data (not your BTC address).

2. BTC auto-conversion

Provider sells the equivalent amount of BTC instantly.

3. Fiat rails

Visa/Mastercard process the transaction normally.

4. Settlement

Merchant receives fiat. Your BTC balance decreases.

Fees and what to check

Fee type Impact
Conversion spread Often the biggest hidden cost in BTC cards.
Transaction fee Some charge per conversion.
Withdrawal/ATM fee Crypto cards often charge more here.
Network fees BTC network congestion can increase costs.
Limits Daily spending/withdrawal limits vary widely.
