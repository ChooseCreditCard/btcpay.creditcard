How Bitcoin Payment Gateways Connect to Card Networks

Bitcoin payment gateways make it possible for merchants to accept BTC while still settling in fiat. This page explains how BTC flows through the system, how conversion works, and how these processes align with crypto-linked credit cards.

Compare crypto-friendly credit cards

What Is a Bitcoin Payment Gateway?

Bitcoin payment gateways allow merchants to accept BTC from customers while still receiving local currency in their bank account. They bridge the gap between crypto payments, market pricing engines and traditional settlement infrastructure.

Instead of merchants managing wallets or exchange risk, the gateway handles rate quotes, invoicing, BTC conversion and settlement. For consumers, these systems often work in parallel with crypto-linked cards that convert BTC at the moment of purchase.

How BTCPay Transactions Move Through the System

When a customer pays in Bitcoin, the gateway generates a payment request with a fixed-time price quote. Once the BTC reaches the gateway’s address, it is consolidated and exchanged for fiat through the provider’s liquidity partners.

The merchant then receives a normal bank transfer. This model mirrors aspects of crypto credit-card payments, where BTC is sold behind the scenes and converted into the fiat needed to settle over traditional card rails.

Fees, Rates and Conversion Timing

Gateways typically charge a combination of processing fees and spreads on BTC conversion. Pricing may shift depending on the size of the transaction, volatility at the time of payment and network congestion fees.

Some gateways lock exchange rates for a short window, while others convert immediately once a transaction is confirmed on-chain. The timing can influence the effective rate paid by the merchant or user.

Rewards, Rebates and Platform Incentives

Unlike crypto credit cards, Bitcoin payment gateways usually do not provide direct rewards. However, certain providers offer fee discounts or rebates for high-volume merchants or users who stake a native platform token.

These structures are separate from credit-card rewards but can interact indirectly when gateways and crypto-linked cards are used within the same ecosystem.

Compliance, Settlement and Recordkeeping

Because BTC payments may count as taxable disposals or capital gains events, robust reporting tools are essential. Gateways often provide detailed settlement statements, transaction histories and downloadable logs for accounting purposes.

For businesses using both Bitcoin gateways and crypto-linked credit cards, clear reporting helps reconcile BTC inflows, fiat settlements and conversion events across systems.
